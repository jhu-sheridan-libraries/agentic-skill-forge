## Editorial Authority

- Apply JHU Editorial Style Guide first.
- Use AP style for points not addressed by JHU.
- Use Chicago only where JHU points to it or where AP does not resolve the issue.
- Use Webster's New World College Dictionary when AP points to a dictionary; choose the first spelling when two are listed.
- Respect unit-specific style guides when supplied, especially Medicine or school-level guidance.

## Names And Capitalization

- Prefer `Johns Hopkins` as the shortened name for Johns Hopkins University.
- Avoid `Hopkins` alone unless `Johns Hopkins` has appeared repeatedly and the context is informal or unmistakable.
- Use `JHU` only for informal or internal communications, or where space constraints make it appropriate.
- In running copy, write `the Johns Hopkins University`, not `The Johns Hopkins University`, unless the name stands alone in a program, invitation, heading, or display context.
- On second reference, lowercase common nouns that replace formal names: `the university`, `the hospital`, `the health system`, `the school`, `the institute`, `the center`.
- Avoid collective legacy terms such as `Johns Hopkins Institutions`, `Johns Hopkins Medical Institutions`, and `Homewood Schools`; name the relevant entities instead.
- For two-entity references, name both entities clearly, such as `Johns Hopkins University and Medicine` or `the Johns Hopkins University and Health System`.
- Use formal division names on first reference when precision matters. Accept shortened forms only after the formal name is clear.

## Acronyms And Naming

- Do not introduce acronyms by putting them in parentheses after a full name. If an acronym is not clear without that construction, avoid it or introduce it naturally later.
- Avoid acronyms in program, event, campaign, and initiative names. They slow comprehension and can create accessibility and translation issues.
- Use straightforward keywords in names. Do not merge words into branded compounds unless a legacy name already requires it.
- Use `Hopkins` or `JHU` in a program or campaign name only when the effort is enterprisewide or the context requires it.

## Voice And Tone

JHU voice characteristics: strong, bright, useful, true.

- Strong: be declarative, succinct, purposeful, and grounded in knowledge.
- Bright: be optimistic, active, and possibility-oriented without hype.
- Useful: use facts and relatable language; avoid jargon, obtuse names, and broad statements.
- True: do not inflate claims; fact-check and source claims where appropriate; follow editorial style consistently.
- Prefer active voice and strong verbs.
- Use short sentences and do not bury the lede.
- Replace vague institutional self-praise with specific outcomes, evidence, audience benefits, or plain description.

## AI And Editorial Integrity

- Treat AI as assistive, not autonomous.
- Do not present AI-generated copy as final public-facing JHU copy without human review.
- Do not fabricate facts, quotes, sources, approvals, or claims about JHU.
- Flag claims that need sourcing or subject-matter verification.
- Avoid saying a piece is officially approved, compliant, or on-brand unless an authorized reviewer has made that determination.
- For public-facing editorial or marketing copy, provide edits and rationale; keep a human editor central to final judgment.

## Common JHU Style Watchlist

- `healthcare`: one word in all ordinary uses. Preserve two words only in formal titles, legislation, or proper names that use it.
- `HIPAA`: avoid where possible; use plain language such as `privacy laws` or `the federal law restricting release of medical information`. Explain it if used in a quote.
- `historic` and `historical`: use `a`, not `an`.
- `homepage`: one word.
- `web`, `website`: lowercase; do not italicize or quote website names.
- `state abbreviations`: spell out state names in running copy.
- `times`: prefer `noon` and `midnight` over `12 p.m.` and `12 a.m.`; do not use `:00` with `o'clock`; use `ET`, not `EST` or `EDT`, when a time zone is needed.
- `The`: lowercase in running copy even when part of most formal names; retain capitalized `The` for newspapers, magazines, and books.
- `theater`: use American spelling unless the formal name uses `Theatre`.
- `titled`: use `titled`, not `entitled`, for works.
- `startup`: one word as a noun or adjective; `start up` as a verb.
- `-wide`: usually no hyphen, as in `universitywide` and `campuswide`.
- `student-athlete`: hyphenate.
- `underway`: one word.
- `work-study`: hyphenate.

## Web And Social Copy

- Web copy should be accessible, mobile-aware, and clear about the unit name; do not rely on acronyms alone.
- JHU.edu sites must represent official business functions clearly and consistently.
- Social copy should be professional, clear, positive, supportive, active, and free of cliches and jargon.
- For social account naming, include `Johns Hopkins` or `Johns Hopkins University` when possible; use `JHU` only if space is limited.

## Review Output

When reviewing, group comments by priority:

- `Must fix`: incorrect JHU naming/style, factual risk, unsupported claim, inaccessible acronym, misleading AI-generated phrasing.
- `Should fix`: wordiness, passive voice, jargon, generic institutional language, unclear audience benefit.
- `Consider`: tone refinement, optional concision, channel-specific polish.

Give suggested replacement language whenever possible.
