## Asset Handling Rule

- Do not bundle, recreate, trace, redraw, recolor, crop into a new mark, or approximate official JHU logos, shields, seal, mascot marks, spirit marks, lock-ups, patterns, icons, photos, videos, templates, or music.
- Direct users to the JHU Download Library for official files. The library includes assets such as logos, patterns, icons, color swatches, shields, spirit marks, templates, wallpapers, photo/video, and campaign assets.
- Before using a downloaded asset, review the matching usage page and restrictions.
- If the user provides an approved asset file for a specific project, preserve the file exactly. Do not edit the asset itself; only place it according to the official guidance.
- If a requested use is unclear, brand-sensitive, co-branded, merchandise-related, legal/official, or public-facing at scale, advise review by University Communications or the appropriate divisional communications office.

## Primary Logo

- Treat the primary Johns Hopkins University logo as the core visual identity.
- Use only digital artwork provided by University Communications through the brand site.
- Do not alter any logo element.
- Do not create custom logos or shields.
- Do not type the wordmark; it is a custom letterform.
- Do not separate the wordmark from the shield or break the logo into parts.
- Use vertical or horizontal logo artwork as supplied.
- Division logos follow the same primary logo and shield rules.

## Logo Color

- Use JHU logos and lock-ups only in approved logo colors: Heritage Blue, white, or black.
- Do not recolor a blue logo to make a white logo.
- Do not recolor a white logo to make a blue logo.
- Do not alter logo files to create unapproved colors.
- Use the Heritage Blue logo on white or light backgrounds.
- Use the white reversed logo on dark backgrounds.
- Use black logo variants only for black-and-white applications such as newsprint.
- Keep division shield color variants distinct; blue and white versions are not interchangeable.

## Logo Background, Clear Space, And Size

- Ensure the logo is legible on the chosen background.
- Do not place the logo on complex patterns, low-contrast backgrounds, or busy photographs.
- Prefer solid backgrounds, simple patterns, high contrast, or photos with negative space.
- Maintain clear space around the logo. The minimum clear space is based on the height of the capital `H` in `Hopkins`; no text or graphic may encroach on this area.
- Do not create unofficial lock-ups by placing text or graphics too close to the logo.
- Do not size the vertical logo below `1.25 in` or `160 px` wide.
- Do not size the horizontal logo below `1.625 in` or `190 px` wide.
- For embroidery, do not size the vertical logo below `3 in` wide or the horizontal logo below `3.5 in` wide.

## Placement

- It is best practice for a primary logo to appear on the initial view of print, digital, and video communications.
- Video communications should generally include the logo at the beginning and end.
- Social-only video may omit the initial view if necessary for quick engagement, but should still include the logo at the end.
- Static social photo posts from official Johns Hopkins accounts do not always need a logo because the account name and profile image provide nearby brand context.

## Shield Mark And Shield Tag

- Shields may be used as design elements without the `Johns Hopkins University` wordmark.
- Keep shields intact; do not alter them.
- Cropping the shield mark is allowed, but do not change the mark itself.
- Apply the same color guidance as the primary logo to shield marks.
- The shield mark may be used as a background element; the brand site recommends `20%` opacity for that use.
- The shield may be combined with typography only for limited-use artwork such as unique merchandise graphics.
- Do not combine the shield with department or division names in a way that creates an alternate logo.
- Use only provided shield tag files.
- When the shield tag is used, the full primary or division logo must also be present in the design, such as in a website footer.
- The shield tag should not be used without the primary logo in close proximity.
- Shield tag clear space: top bleed allowance should ideally equal the shield height; left, right, and bottom clear space should be `25%` of tag width.
- Shield tag colors: Heritage Blue preferred; Medium Blue and white are secondary options for dark backgrounds.

## Seal, Spirit Marks, Blue Jay Marks, And Mascot Marks

- The university seal requires permission from University Communications and may never be altered or varied.
- The full-color seal is reserved for official documents, presidential/trustee materials, legal or academic documentation, and highest awards/certificates.
- Spirit marks may not replace the primary logo on any communication.
- Spirit marks may not be altered or combined with other graphics or text.
- The Split Block H may not be altered, locked with logos/text, or have its color division, proportion, or font changed.
- Use only one Blue Jay Mark in a design. Across a design series, multiple approved Blue Jay Marks may be used.
- Blue Jay Marks may be cropped but not otherwise altered.
- Do not lock text or graphics to Blue Jay Marks except in licensed merchandise design.
- Custom blue jay illustrations and iconography are not allowed without permission from University Communications.
- The Shielded Blue Jay is reserved for Johns Hopkins Athletics.
- Mascot Marks must remain full color; no additional colorways are available.
- Only University Communications may alter Mascot Marks.
- Custom mascot illustrations are not permitted.
- Avoid images of the old mascot costume; the costume was updated in 2019.
- Baby Jay is for youth merchandise and youth/family programming collateral; other uses require University Communications approval.

## Co-Branding

- For internal co-sponsorship, use the Johns Hopkins University logo and list unit or division names separately in text.
- Do not use more than one division logo, unit lock-up, or interdisciplinary lock-up in the same design space.
- Do not combine multiple unit names into one unit lock-up.
- For external co-branding, use the primary Johns Hopkins University logo to represent the institution unless official guidance says otherwise.
- Only one Johns Hopkins logo may be used at a time.
- If multiple JHU divisions are equal partners in an external event, use the primary Johns Hopkins University logo and recognize individual units in text.
- When sharing materials with external partners, ensure correct files are shared and brand guidelines are followed.
- Use partner marks only with permission.
- Partner logos must be high-resolution and have transparent backgrounds; if a partner logo is blurry, list the partner name in text instead.
- When JHU is an equal partner, use the JHU logo with adequate clear space and equal proportion to partner logos.

## Color And Accessibility Notes

- Use official color values from the Colors page, not Pantone Color Bridge approximations.
- Always use listed hex values for web/digital work because JHU adjusts some web values for accessibility.
- Primary colors:
  - Heritage Blue: PMS 288 C; CMYK `100, 80, 6, 32`; RGB `0, 45, 114`; HEX `#002D72`.
  - Spirit Blue: PMS 284 C; CMYK `56, 18, 0, 0`; RGB `104, 172, 229`; HEX `#68ACE5`.
- At least one primary color, preferably Heritage Blue, should appear frequently and prominently across communications and platforms.
- Secondary colors should be used intentionally and sparingly, about `20%` of the total color.
- Gradients should be at least `50%` Heritage Blue, use only primary and cool accent colors, and use no more than three colors.
- Follow WCAG 2.1 Level AA contrast: `4.5:1` for normal text, `3:1` for large text, and `3:1` for graphics and UI components.
- Harbor Blue with white is for large text only.
- Spirit Blue is not accessible with white text or a white background.

## Typography Notes

- Current open-source brand typefaces: Work Sans, Roboto Slab, Source Serif 4, and Oswald.
- Do not bundle font files in this editorial skill. Direct users to official guidance and Google Fonts.
- Adobe Garamond is strictly reserved for the Johns Hopkins University logo system. Do not use it for marketing materials or to retype the logo.

## Review Checklist

Use this checklist when a user asks about official JHU assets:

1. Is the asset official artwork from the Download Library or provided by an authorized JHU source?
2. Is the requested asset the correct one for the audience, unit, channel, and partnership context?
3. Is the asset unaltered?
4. Are color, background, contrast, clear space, and minimum size rules satisfied?
5. Is any shield, spirit mark, mascot mark, seal, or co-branded use within its allowed context?
6. Are partner marks used only with permission and at acceptable quality?
7. Does the request need review by University Communications or a divisional communications office?
