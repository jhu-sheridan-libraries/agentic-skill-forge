# AI Trope Cleanup

Use this guide when copy sounds generic, over-polished, inflated, evasive, or "LLM-written." The goal is not to make copy flatter; it is to make it more specific, human, and useful.

## Common Tropes To Challenge

Treat these as clues, not automatic errors:

- `in today's fast-paced world`
- `in an ever-evolving landscape`
- `now more than ever`
- `it is important to note`
- `it is worth noting`
- `at its core`
- `when it comes to`
- `from X to Y`
- `whether you are X or Y`
- `delve into`
- `dive into`
- `unlock`
- `harness`
- `leverage`
- `empower`
- `elevate`
- `foster`
- `drive innovation`
- `cutting-edge`
- `game-changing`
- `groundbreaking` without evidence
- `transformative` without evidence
- `robust`
- `seamless` or `seamlessly`
- `dynamic`
- `comprehensive`
- `tailored`
- `holistic`
- `vibrant`
- `rich tapestry`
- `beacon`
- `testament to`
- `underscore`
- `pave the way`
- `reimagine`
- `revolutionize`
- `mission-critical`
- `best-in-class`
- `world-class` without support

## Rewrite Moves

- Replace setup throat-clearing with the main point.
- Replace abstract claims with concrete benefits, facts, dates, people, places, or actions.
- Replace hype adjectives with verifiable nouns and verbs.
- Replace "we are committed to" with what JHU actually does or will do.
- Replace "leverage/utilize" with `use` unless the technical sense is intended.
- Replace "empower" with the actual capability or opportunity.
- Replace "foster" with `build`, `support`, `encourage`, `create`, or the precise action.
- Replace "delve/dive into" with `explore`, `examine`, `study`, or a direct verb.
- Cut duplicated modifiers, paired abstractions, and generic transitions.
- Prefer one strong sentence over a polished but empty paragraph.

## JHU-Appropriate Voice Targets

- Strong: direct, concise, confident.
- Bright: optimistic without exaggeration.
- Useful: audience-specific and plainspoken.
- True: fact-checked, sourced where needed, and free of inflated claims.

## Before And After Patterns

Weak:
`In today's rapidly evolving landscape, Johns Hopkins is leveraging cutting-edge research to drive transformative change.`

Stronger:
`Johns Hopkins researchers are developing new tools to help clinicians identify risk earlier.`

Weak:
`This dynamic event will empower attendees to delve into a rich tapestry of ideas.`

Stronger:
`At the event, students will compare research methods, hear from faculty mentors, and meet potential collaborators.`

Weak:
`Our comprehensive program fosters innovation and unlocks opportunities for future leaders.`

Stronger:
`The program pairs students with faculty mentors, seed funding, and workshops on moving an idea from prototype to pilot.`

## Review Notes

- Do not remove all warmth or ambition. Keep claims vivid when they are specific and supported.
- Preserve domain terms when the audience expects them; define or simplify them when writing for a broader audience.
- Leave quotes intact unless asked to edit them.
- When a trope appears in an official title, proper noun, or quotation, flag it only if the user can change it.
